Masks Required Statewide

Masks, face coverings or face shields are currently required statewide for all workplaces and indoor public spaces (for example offices, grocery stores, pharmacies, public transit, personal services providers, restaurants, bars, retail stores and more). Masks, face coverings or face shields are also required at outdoor businesses and in outdoor public spaces when physical distancing of at least six feet is not possible. Children age 5 and older are required to wear a face covering. People with a disability or medical condition may request accommodation from a business, public space or workspace if they cannot wear one.


Flu shots:
Don't wait
to vaccinate

Flu shots are more important than ever this year.

Getting a flu shot is one simple thing people can do to keep themselves and their loved ones healthy this fall and winter. It’s more important than ever as we continue to fight COVID-19.

While getting a flu shot won’t protect you from COVID-19, it’s a safe and effective way to prevent the flu. Plus it helps essential workers stay healthy and it preserves medical resources for patients with COVID-19.

Everyone 6 months and older should get a flu shot, especially people with chronic health conditions, older adults, pregnant women, health care workers and children.

The flu vaccine may take up to two weeks to become effective, so getting it earlier in the season is ideal.

COVID-19 Weekly Report: slight drop in weekly cases

COVID-19 Weekly Report

Oct. 21, 2020 - The latest Weekly Report showed that during the week of Oct. 12 through Oct. 18, OHA recorded 2,327 new cases of COVID-19 infection—down 4% from last week’s tally of 2,418.

The number of Oregonians newly tested rose 28,960, and the percentage of tests that were positive rose slightly to 6.5% from 6.4% the prior week. Twenty-seven Oregonians were reported to have died in association with COVID-19—compared to 27 last week. One hundred forty-three Oregonians were hospitalized.

Halloween: Celebrating safely

Consider the risk of the activities you choose

People in Oregon are creative, they care about their community and they know it’s important to celebrate safely. This year, it’s more important than ever to put safety first because COVID-19 cases have risen recently and holiday gatherings on Memorial Day, July 4th and Labor Day led to increased case counts.

So, this Halloween, be extra mindful of your choices. Choosing low risk Halloween plans can help prevent the spread of COVID-19, decrease the impact on Oregon’s health care system and save lives.

Planning ahead for a COVID-19 vaccine

Planning ahead for a COVID-19 vaccine

The CDC has asked all states to share how they plan to distribute COVID-19 vaccine once a safe and effective vaccine is ready. OHA has submitted a draft plan to CDC.

This draft plan is only the first step. OHA is reaching out to public health, health care and community organizations to partner with us as we continue to develop this plan. We want to understand how our current systems for getting people vaccinated may need to be rebuilt to meet the needs of communities who are disproportionately impacted by COVID-19.

OHA’s plan will follow federal guidance for a phased approach. This means starting with vaccinations for critical groups, including people involved in the pandemic response and people at the highest risk for getting very sick. As more vaccine becomes available, there will be wider distribution to other high risk groups and the general public.

Watch List updated

Lane County added to Watch List

October 16, 2020 - Gov. Kate Brown announced today that Lane County has been added to the County Watch List, bringing the total number of counties on the Watch List to five.

Counties are placed on the Watch List when COVID-19 is spreading quickly and public health officials can't trace that spread to specific sources, which indicates community spread. The state prioritizes resources and assistance to these counties that are seeing the broadest spread of COVID-19. Counties remain on the Watch List for a minimum of three weeks and until their sporadic case rate drops below 50 per 100,000 or 5 or fewer sporadic cases in the previous two weeks.

The complete County Watch List now includes the following five counties: Benton, Clatsop, Lane, Malheur, and Umatilla.
